Off the beaten path

Big Ben, London Eye, the Tower of London, and Trafalgar Square are renowned landmarks in London that you must visit, but I'd encourage you to explore beyond these famous attractions. You'll be pleasantly surprised by the abundance of hidden treasures that are less touristy. London has an array of historical sights, trendy shopping spots, and various other delightful places waiting to be discovered. If you're looking for some unique experiences on your next visit, here are some of them.

SPOTS

My favourite places in London

Kew Gardens

Kew Gardens is a botanic garden in southwest London that houses the largest and most diverse botanical and mycological collections in the world.

Address

Visitors can access Kew Gardens via four entrance gates: Victoria Gate (TW9 3JR) Elizabeth Gate (TW9 3AB) Brentford Gate (TW9 3AF)

What I like about it

Temperate House. It's the world's largest Victorian glasshouse.


Learn more

The Barbican Centre

A leading London art complex, and a prime example of brutalist architecture. The centre hosts concerts, theatre performances, film screenings and art exhibitions.

Address

Beech Street Barbican London EC2Y 8AE

What I like about it

A leading UK arts complex, it houses a cinema, art gallery, theatre, dedicated workshop and learning spaces, and its own glorious conservatory where you can take afternoon tea.


Learn more

Daunt Books, Marylebone

Everyone loves an old bookshop and Daunt Books is everything a bookshop lover would wish for, and one of my favourite hidden spots in London.

Address

84 Marylebone High St, London W1U 4QW

What I like about it

The legacy of Francis Edwards can still be felt in Daunt Books today through its stunning interiors which stayed true to the original building. The heart of the bookshop is filled by a long galleried main room which is spectacularly decorated with a beautiful arched window.
